Cloud is supposed to remove undifferentiated heavy lifting… why are there so many *Ops ?

You’ve probably heard of DevOps — the combination of Development practices and IT operations to shortern the software development lifecycle. It was the original of all the “Ops” disciplines.

Just when you thought it the cloud was supposed to make it easier for all — there seems to be more operations than you can point a stick at!

Here’s just a few of them…..

  1. GreenOps: Running sustainable (or at least carbon-netural) cloud environments…
  2. DataOps: An automated, process-oriented methodology, used by analytic and data teams, to improve the quality and reduce the cycle time of data analytics.
  3. SecOps (or DevSecOps): Refers to integrating security practices within the DevOps process. SecOps involves collaboration between security, operations, and development teams.
  4. FinOps: Aims to bring financial accountability to the variable spending model of cloud, enabling teams to make business trade-offs between speed, cost, and quality.
  5. GitOps: An operational framework that takes DevOps best practices used for application development such as version control, collaboration, compliance, and CI/CD, and applies them to infrastructure automation.
  6. AIOps: Utilizes big data, data analytics, and machine learning to automate IT operations processes, including event correlation, anomaly detection, and causality determination.
  7. ChatOps: Leverages chat clients, chatbots, and real-time communication tools to facilitate how software development and operation tasks are communicated and executed.
  8. MLOps (or AIOps): Applying DevOps principles to machine learning algorithms. This includes continuous integration, testing, deployment, and monitoring of ML models.
  9. NoOps: The anti-ops! If you have too many ops, this is the one where we just stop doing it. I jest, but really it is the notion where you shouldn’t need to operate anything as the infrastructure is abstrated away that you shouldn’t need a dedicated operations team.
  10. BizOps: Integrates business IT systems with line-of-business or end-user systems to help organizations run smoothly. BizOps can also refer to a team or organizational function that uses data, analytics, and technology to deliver optimal business performance.
  11. HROps (or PeopleOps): Human Resources Operations, it focuses on creating a great employee experience using data and HR technology.
  12. SalesOps: Really? Is this really a thing? surely this is just product-based selling (y’know, the usual SaaS selling model). However, Apprently it is a real thing! Increasing sales productivity through reduced friction in the sales process through technology, process improvement and reporting.
